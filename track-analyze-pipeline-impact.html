Track and analyze pipeline impact

In Uptempo Campaign Management, impact modeling helps you to demonstrate the impact of your activities on the demand pipeline, and links your marketing efforts to business results.

On your Uptempo activities, you can record the demand you expect the activity to generate. The system uses the planned demand numbers you enter to calculate projections of how your initiatives are likely to influence revenue over time. This makes it easier to align marketing plans with organizational goals, and to communicate expected value to stakeholders.

Impact modeling also supports better decision-making during planning. By making the expected impact of activities visible upfront, you can prioritize activities with the greatest potential return, and adjust plans before resources are committed. As execution progresses, the impact model provides a reference point for evaluating whether activities are performing as expected, and where corrective action may be needed.

How impact modeling works

During the planning phase, you can enter the estimated number of generated inquiries or leads that you're targeting for an activity. Your estimated demand numbers are then fed into a detailed model of your demand pipeline, which uses them to project anticipated revenue from each activity, and when that revenue is expected to be realized.

During the execution phase, you can record actual results for inquiries or leads created by the same activities, as well as for revenue generated by closed deals. By comparing targets and results in real time, you can quickly see what’s working and what’s not, so that you can make informed decisions about how to adjust your strategy.

You can enter impact data for planned targets and actual results at any level of your activity hierarchy. All impact data automatically aggregates up to the top of the activity hierarchy along hierarchy paths, so you can get full insight into performance at every level of your marketing plan.

About the pipeline impact model

Your Uptempo administrator sets up the pipeline impact model that the system uses to generate projections. The impact model consists of the following parts:

  • The structure of your organization's demand generation funnel. This consists of the stages that you track as customers move through the sales pipeline, from lead to closed/won deal.

  • Your baseline funnel performance metrics. This includes typical conversion rates (the rate at which leads move from one funnel stage to the next), velocities (how fast they move between stages), and the average value of a closed/won deal.

  • Segment-specific custom metrics to enhance the baseline metrics. These provide alternative, more specific funnel performance assumptions based on a combination of dimensions, such as activity attribute values and activity groups. For example, you can set overrides for scenarios such as if the average deal size for a certain product is higher or lower, or if deals in a particular market close faster (or slower) than average.

When you enter a planned demand target for an activity, Uptempo feeds this input into the pipeline impact model and generates a projection of how much revenue the activity is expected to generate, and when. To do this, it uses segment-specific metrics if available (determined by the attributes set on the activity), or otherwise uses the baseline performance metrics if there is no applicable segment.

View activity impact data

For each activity, the Impact section of the activity's details panel contains its impact modeling data. Here, you can view existing impact data and projections, as well as enter planned and actual impact results.

View an activity's impact data

  1. In the Activities section, click on the activity for which you want to view impact data. The activity's details panel opens.

  2. In the details panel, click the Impact section to open it.

  3. The Impact section contains two tabs:

Planned

Use this tab to enter and view a planned target for demand generated by the activity.

Actual

Use this tab to enter and view actual results for the demand and revenue attribute to the activity, and compare them to the planned targets.

The Impact section does not appear in an activity's details panel if it is not enabled for that activity type.

    Planned demand targets and projected impact

    You enter the target demand that you plan to generate in the Planned tab of an activity's Impact section. After you enter a target, the impact model displays a projection of how the planned demand is expected to influence revenue over the time period during which the activity is in-market.

    You can enter targets at any level of the activity hierarchy. However, for any given hierarchy path, a KPI value can be entered and maintained at only one level. After a value is set at one level, it cannot be entered or edited at other levels along the same path.

    Activities at higher levels automatically display roll-up impact data aggregated from all lower levels and use blended model assumptions from those levels when generating projections. Lower-level (descendant) activities, by contrast, do not display data entered at higher levels or projections derived from that data.

    For this reason, enter demand targets at the lowest level of the activity hierarchy. Doing so ensures that impact data and projections are visible and consistent at every level above.

    Enter planned demand targets for an activity

    Attribute values are taken into account by the impact model, so the specific attribute values set on an activity can influence the projections generated by the model.

    Before you enter demand targets for an activity, make sure you have entered values for all attributes in the Details section of the activity's details panel.

    1. In the Activities section, click on the activity for which you want to enter planned demand targets. The activity's details panel opens.

    2. In the details panel, click the Impact section.

    3. Click the Planned tab.

    4. Enter the planned demand target (for example, the number of inquiries you expect the activity to generate) into the input field:

      The name of the input field is based on the first stage of your organization's demand funnel, so it may have a different name in your Uptempo instance than what is shown here.

    5. Click anywhere outside the input field to stop editing it.

    The planned demand target you entered is saved automatically, and is used to generate an impact projection for the activity.

      Adjust how planned demand is distributed

      By default, the impact model projection assumes that the planned demand is generated evenly across the period defined by the activity's in-market dates. For activities with longer in-market periods, the generated demand might be unevenly distributed within the in-market period. For example, you might see a peak in demand during the holiday period followed by a dip afterward, with a corresponding peak and dip in revenue.

      To generate more accurate revenue projections for these situations, you can adjust how the planned demand is distributed across the in-market period.

      Adjust planned demand distribution for an activity

      1. On the activity's details panel, go to Impact > Planned.

      2. If you haven't done so yet, enter the planned demand target for the activity into the input field.

      3. In the Distribute Results menu, choose the time granularity you want to use when distributing the planned demand target:

        Monthly/Periodically

        Enter separate demand target figures for each month or fiscal year period in the in-market period.

        Quarterly

        Enter separate demand target figures for each quarter in the in-market period.

        None

        Enter a single demand target figure for the entire in-market period.

      4. Additional input fields appear according to the time granularity you chose. If the in-market period spans multiple years, a control to switch between the years is also displayed:

      5. To adjust the planned demand targets in each input field, click Edit distribution. The input fields are unlocked, and you can change their values:

      The planned demand targets you enter are saved automatically.

        View impact projections

        After you enter a planned demand target for an activity, the system uses it to generate and display an impact projection chart in the activity's details panel on the Impact > Planned tab:

        • After you enter planned demand targets, the chart displays line graphs to visualize planned demand (in purple) and planned revenue (in yellow) over time.

        • After you add actual results, the chart also displays line graphs to visualize actual demand (in orange), and actual revenue (in green), for comparison.

        • The time period represented by the chart's x-axis starts with the activity's in-market start date. It ends on the date the revenue from the last closed/won deal is planned to be realized, the date when the last actual revenue was generated, or the current date (whichever is latest).

        To generate an impact projection chart, the system requires:

        • For planned demand and revenue: Planned impact data (on the Impact > Planned tab) and in-market dates (in the Details section).

        • For actual demand and revenue: Actual impact data (on the Impact > Actual tab).

        If you've entered only planned impact data but are not seeing a chart, check that you've also specified in-market dates for the activity (the system needs these dates to plot planned demand and revenue over time).

        Place the pointer on the chart to see the exact value on any line graph at the pointer's position.

        When the system has generated an impact projection, you can also see details about the funnel assumptions that the impact model used to generate the projections.

        View funnel assumptions for an activity's projections

        1. On the activity's details panel, go to Impact > Planned.

        2. Click Show Funnel Assumptions.

        3. A visualization of your demand funnel is displayed:

          • The funnel stage names, and the assumed conversion rates and velocities, are displayed in the center of the funnel diagram

          • The number of leads projected to move on to the next stage are shown on the left of the funnel diagram

          • The projected revenue generated is shown on the right of the funnel diagram

          If your organization tracks revenue generation at multiple stages of the funnel, you will see figures for each stage at which revenue is tracked.

          • The basis for the assumptions used is noted below the funnel diagram.

        4. To hide the funnel assumptions again, click Hide Funnel Assumptions.

          Actual demand and revenue results

          You enter the actual demand and revenue results achieved in the Actual tab of an activity's Impact section. After you enter actual results for a KPI, Uptempo automatically displays them on the impact projection chart (on the Planned tab), so you can visualize how the activity performed compared to the projections:

          Enter actual demand and revenue results for an activity

          1. In the Activities section, click on the activity for which you want to enter actual demand and revenue results. The activity's details panel opens.

          2. In the details panel, click the Impact section.

          3. Click the Actual tab and scroll down to the input table.

          4. Optional: Use the Time Granularity menu to select the time granularity you want to use when entering results:

            Total

            Enter results as a single total figure for the entire in-market period. This is the default setting.

            Monthly/Periodically

            Enter separate results figures for each month or fiscal year period in the in-market period.

            Quarterly

            Enter separate results figures for quarter in the in-market period.

            If you select Monthly/Periodically or Quarterly, additional rows are added to the table according to the granularity you chose. Click Add month/quarter to add more table rows.

          5. Enter your actual demand and revenue results into the table. There are table columns for each stage of your demand funnel, and for each stage at which you track revenue:

          6. To save your demand and revenue results, click Save.

          7. The results you entered are displayed in aggregated form on the Actual tab, with a color-coded percentage comparison to the planned/projected figures:
