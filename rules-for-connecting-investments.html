Rules for connecting investments

In Uptempo, you can connect investments to your activities. However, because both activities and investments are organized into hierarchies, not all activities can be connected to all investments. To prevent funding conflicts and invalid connections, Uptempo automatically applies the following rules whenever activities and investments are connected.

To understand these rules, it's helpful to know how hierarchical relationships and paths work in Uptempo. For details, see Hierarchy paths and relationships.

Rule 1: An activity can only be connected once within a path of the investment hierarchy

There can only be one connection between an activity and any investment within a given path of the investment hierarchy.

Rule 2: Connections must follow ancestor-to-ancestor and descendant-to-descendant relationships

When a path in the activity hierarchy is connected to a path in the investment hierarchy, additional connections must respect the relative positions of activities and investments in their respective hierarchies.

Connected paths restrict further connections

After the initial connection between an activity hierarchy path and an investment hierarchy path is made, all additional connections must stay within those paths. Connections outside the established paths are not allowed.

As a result, the initial connections between activities and investments are critical: they determine which paths are available for all subsequent connections.

Rule 3: The highest connected activity can be connected to a different investment plan

Within a single investment plan, the first connection determines the available paths for subsequent connections, as described in Rule 2. However, you can also make connections to additional investment plans.

Only the highest connected activity in the activity hierarchy can be connected to another investment plan.
